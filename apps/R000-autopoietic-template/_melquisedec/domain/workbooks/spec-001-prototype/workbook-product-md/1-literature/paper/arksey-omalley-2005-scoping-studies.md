## 1. Contexto y Motivación del Paper

### 1.1. Problema Identificado

**Context (circa 2005)**:
- **Systematic Reviews** eran gold standard en evidence-based practice
- **Scoping Studies** emergían como alternativa, pero sin metodología consistente
- Términos usados intercambiablemente sin definición clara: "scoping review", "scoping exercise", "mapping review"

**Gap en Literatura**:
- No había framework metodológico establecido para Scoping Studies
- Diferentes autores usaban distintos approaches sin justificación
- Falta de guidance sobre cuándo usar Scoping Study vs Systematic Review

**Objetivo del Paper**:
> "To clarify the definition, purpose and methodological framework for undertaking scoping studies, and to distinguish them from systematic reviews."

## 2. Definición de Scoping Study

### 2.1. Definición según Arksey & O'Malley

**Scoping Study**:
> "A technique to 'map' relevant literature in the field of interest. This form of knowledge synthesis addresses an exploratory research question aimed at mapping key concepts, types of evidence, and gaps in research related to a defined area or field by systematically searching, selecting, and synthesizing existing knowledge."

**Características Clave**:
- **Exploratory**: No busca responder pregunta específica (como Systematic Review)
- **Mapping**: Identifica landscape de literatura existente
- **Breadth over depth**: Cubre amplio rango, no profundiza en calidad de estudios
- **Iterative**: Proceso puede refinarse conforme se descubre literatura

### 2.2. Scoping Study vs Systematic Review

| Aspecto | Scoping Study | Systematic Review |
|---------|--------------|------------------|
| **Research Question** | Broad, exploratory | Narrow, specific |
| **Purpose** | Map extent/range/nature of evidence | Answer specific question with best evidence |
| **Quality Appraisal** | ❌ Typically NOT included | ✅ Critical appraisal mandatory |
| **Synthesis** | Narrative/descriptive | Meta-analysis (quantitative) or narrative synthesis |
| **Time** | Faster (weeks-months) | Slower (months-years) |
| **Team Size** | Smaller (1-2 reviewers OK) | Larger (2+ reviewers mandatory) |
| **Protocol** | Flexible, iterative | Rigid, pre-registered |

**Ejemplo Comparativo**:
- **Systematic Review Question**: "¿Cuál es la efectividad de intervención X para reducir outcome Y en población Z?" (específica, PICO framework)
- **Scoping Study Question**: "¿Qué se sabe sobre intervenciones para outcome Y en diferentes poblaciones?" (exploratoria, sin PICO estricto)

### 2.3. Cuándo Usar Scoping Study

**Situaciones Apropiadas** (según Arksey & O'Malley):

1. **Examinar extent, range, nature de investigación**:
   - Cuando se desconoce tamaño del cuerpo de literatura
   - Para identificar si Systematic Review es factible

2. **Determinar value de undertaking Systematic Review**:
   - Precursor de Systematic Review (scoping primero para ver si vale la pena)

3. **Summarize y disseminate findings**:
   - Proveer overview para practitioners/policymakers

4. **Identify research gaps**:
   - Descubrir áreas donde se necesita más investigación
   - Informar future research agenda

**Situaciones NO Apropiadas**:
- ❌ Cuando se necesita respuesta definitiva a pregunta específica → Systematic Review
- ❌ Cuando se requiere meta-análisis cuantitativo → Systematic Review
- ❌ Cuando se busca "best evidence" con quality appraisal → Systematic Review

## 3. Framework Metodológico: 6 Stages

Arksey & O'Malley proponen **6 stages** para conducir Scoping Study:

### Stage 1: Identifying the Research Question

**Objetivo**: Definir pregunta de investigación amplia que guiará scoping.

**Características de Buena Research Question**:
- **Broad**: Permite cubrir amplio rango de literatura
- **Clear**: Específica suficiente para guiar búsqueda
- **Exploratory**: No presupone findings

**Ejemplos del Paper**:
- ✅ "What is known from the existing literature about services for X population?"
- ✅ "What types of interventions have been used for X outcome?"
- ❌ "Is intervention X effective for outcome Y?" (demasiado específica → Systematic Review)

**Proceso de Refinamiento**:
- Pregunta inicial puede ser muy amplia
- Se refina iterativamente conforme se familiariza con literatura
- **Iterative nature**: OK cambiar pregunta durante scoping (diferente de Systematic Review)

**Ejemplo Aplicación DAATH-ZEN**:
- **Initial Question**: "¿Qué frameworks existen para definir product vision?"
- **Refined Question** (después de explorar): "¿Cómo definen product vision los frameworks de product management, y qué componentes incluyen?"

### Stage 2: Identifying Relevant Studies

**Objetivo**: Identificar estudios relevantes de manera comprehensiva.

#### 2.1. Developing Search Strategy

**Componentes**:
1. **Search Terms**: Keywords + synonyms
2. **Databases**: Electronic databases a consultar
3. **Date Range**: Timeframe de búsqueda
4. **Language**: Restricción de idioma (si aplica)

**Ejemplo del Paper**:
- Search terms: "service provision" OR "service delivery" OR "support services"
- Databases: MEDLINE, CINAHL, PsycINFO, Social Sciences Citation Index
- Date range: 1980-2004
- Language: English only

#### 2.2. Types of Sources

**Arksey & O'Malley recomiendan**:
1. **Electronic databases**: PubMed, Scopus, Web of Science
2. **Reference lists**: Snowballing (citar referencias de papers encontrados)
3. **Hand-searching**: Key journals relevantes
4. **Grey literature**: Reports, theses, conference proceedings
5. **Organizations**: Websites de organizaciones relevantes
6. **Personal contacts**: Experts en el campo

**Breadth vs Depth Trade-off**:
- Scoping Study prioriza **breadth** (cubrir amplio rango)
- No exhaustivo como Systematic Review (OK no encontrar todo)
- Pero debe ser **systematic** (proceso replicable documentado)

**Ejemplo Aplicación DAATH-ZEN**:
- Electronic: Google Scholar ("product vision" OR "product strategy")
- Books: O'Reilly Safari, Amazon (Inspired, Running Lean, Strategize)
- Grey literature: Blog posts (SVPG, Lean Stack, Roman Pichler)
- Organizations: ProductTalk.org, Mind the Product

#### 2.3. Documentation

**Requisito**: Documentar search strategy para replicabilidad

**Información a registrar**:
- Search terms usados (con Boolean operators)
- Databases consultadas + date of search
- Número de results por database
- Criteria para inclusión/exclusión (preliminares)

### Stage 3: Study Selection

**Objetivo**: Seleccionar estudios basados en inclusion/exclusion criteria.

#### 3.1. Inclusion/Exclusion Criteria

**Desarrollar criteria basado en**:
- **Population**: ¿Qué población/contexto?
- **Intervention** (si aplica): ¿Qué tipo de intervención/framework?
- **Outcome**: ¿Qué outcomes/componentes interesan?
- **Study Design**: ¿Qué tipos de estudios? (RCT, qualitative, reviews, etc.)

**Ejemplo del Paper**:
- Inclusion: Studies sobre services for carers, cualquier study design, published 1980-2004
- Exclusion: Studies solo sobre patient outcomes (no carer-focused), non-English

#### 3.2. Selection Process

**Two-Stage Process** (recomendado):

**Stage 3a: Title & Abstract Screening**
- Review titles + abstracts de todos los search results
- Aplicar inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Cuando hay duda, incluir (screening conservador)

**Stage 3b: Full-Text Review**
- Obtener full-text de estudios incluidos en Stage 3a
- Aplicar criteria más rigurosamente
- Documentar reasons para exclusión

**Number of Reviewers**:
- Idealmente 2+ reviewers independientes (como Systematic Review)
- Pero en Scoping Study, 1 reviewer acceptable si recursos limitados
- Resolver discrepancias por discusión o tercer reviewer

**Ejemplo Aplicación DAATH-ZEN**:
- Stage 3a: 150 papers/books encontrados → 40 incluidos (títulos relevantes)
- Stage 3b: 40 full-texts → 15 incluidos (realmente definen product vision con componentes específicos)
- Exclusión: 25 papers mencionan "vision" pero no definen framework

#### 3.3. PRISMA Flow Diagram

**Recomendación de Arksey & O'Malley**: Usar PRISMA-style flow diagram

```

├─ Database search: n = 1,245
├─ Reference lists: n = 38

```

### Stage 4: Charting the Data

**Objetivo**: Extraer información clave de estudios incluidos de manera sistemática.

#### 4.1. Data Charting Form

**Diseñar form** con campos estándar:

**Información Bibliográfica**:
- Author(s), Year, Title
- Journal/Source, Country
- Study type (RCT, qualitative, review, etc.)

**Información Sustantiva**:
- Population/participants
- Intervention/phenomenon of interest
- Key findings/outcomes
- Gaps/limitations identified

**Ejemplo del Paper** (services for carers):
```
| Author | Year | Country | Study Type | Service Type | Key Findings |
|--------|------|---------|------------|--------------|--------------|
| Smith  | 2001 | UK      | Survey     | Respite care | 60% carers unaware of services |
| Jones  | 2003 | USA     | RCT        | Support groups | Reduced carer burden (p<0.05) |
```

#### 4.2. Iterative Nature of Charting

**Diferencia con Systematic Review**:
- En Systematic Review: Data extraction form es fixed desde inicio
- En Scoping Study: Form puede evolucionar conforme se revisan papers

**Proceso**:
1. Diseñar initial charting form
2. Pilot con 5-10 papers
3. Refinar form basado en learnings
4. Re-chart papers piloteados con form final

**Ejemplo Aplicación DAATH-ZEN**:
```
| Framework | Author | Year | Components of Product Vision | Timeframe | Examples |
|-----------|--------|------|------------------------------|-----------|----------|
| Vision Board | Pichler | 2016 | Vision, Target Group, Needs, Product, Goals | 2-5 years | Skype |
| SVPG | Cagan | 2017 | WHO/WHAT/WHY/WHEN/HOW | 2-5 years | Netflix |
| Lean Canvas | Maurya | 2012 | UVP, Customer Segments, Problem | N/A | Airbnb |
```

#### 4.3. Number of Reviewers

- 1 reviewer puede hacer data charting (diferencia con Systematic Review)
- Pero second reviewer recomendado para verificar accuracy (10-20% sample)

### Stage 5: Collating, Summarizing, and Reporting the Results

**Objetivo**: Presentar overview de material revisado.

#### 5.1. Analytical Framework

**Three-Part Structure** (recomendado por Arksey & O'Malley):

**Part 1: Numerical Analysis**
- Descriptive statistics de estudios incluidos
- Distribution por: año, país, study type, journal

**Ejemplo**:
- Total studies: n = 45
- Study types: RCT (n=12), Surveys (n=18), Qualitative (n=15)
- Countries: UK (n=20), USA (n=15), Canada (n=10)
- Years: 1980-1990 (n=5), 1991-2000 (n=15), 2001-2004 (n=25)

**Part 2: Thematic Analysis**
- Identify themes/patterns emergentes
- Group findings por categories

**Ejemplo del Paper** (services for carers):
- Theme 1: Information provision (lack of awareness)
- Theme 2: Respite care (limited availability)
- Theme 3: Support groups (effectiveness varies)
- Theme 4: Financial support (complex eligibility)

**Part 3: Implications**
- Gaps in literature identificados
- Recommendations para future research
- Implications para policy/practice

#### 5.2. Presentation Format

**Tabular Format**:
- Tablas summary de key characteristics
- Facilita quick scanning por lectores

**Narrative Format**:
- Prose describing findings
- Más detail que tablas solas

**Visual Format** (opcional):
- Concept maps mostrando relationships
- Timeline de desarrollo del campo

**Ejemplo Aplicación DAATH-ZEN**:

**Table 1: Frameworks for Product Vision (n=4)**
| Framework | Components | Timeframe | Focus | Source |
|-----------|-----------|-----------|-------|--------|
| Product Vision Board | 5 (Vision, Target, Needs, Product, Goals) | Long-term | Strategic alignment | Pichler 2016 |
| SVPG | 5 (WHO/WHAT/WHY/WHEN/HOW) | 2-5 years | Inspiration | Cagan 2017 |
| Lean Canvas | 3 (UVP, Customer Segments, Problem) | N/A | Business model | Maurya 2012 |
| OKR | 2 (Objectives, Key Results) | Quarterly | Execution | Doerr 2017 |

**Narrative**:
"Four main frameworks emerged for defining product vision. All emphasize customer-centricity (Target Group, WHO, Customer Segments). Timeframes vary from quarterly (OKR) to 2-5 years (SVPG). Product Vision Board is most visual/tactical, SVPG most inspirational/narrative."

#### 5.3. Quality Appraisal (Optional)

**Arksey & O'Malley Position**:
> "Quality appraisal is not typically required for scoping studies, as the goal is to map the breadth of literature rather than synthesize evidence quality."

**Pero**: Algunos autores posteriores (Levac 2010, Peters 2015) argumentan que quality appraisal puede ser útil para identificar gaps en rigor metodológico.

**Recommendation**:
- **Not mandatory** en Scoping Study
- **Consider** si findings revelan alta variabilidad en study quality
- **Include** si goal es identificar "best evidence" dentro del scope

### Stage 6: Consultation (Optional)

**Objetivo**: Complementar literatura con insights de stakeholders.

**Razón para Inclusion**:
> "Consultation provides opportunities for consumer and stakeholder involvement to suggest additional references and provide insights beyond those in the literature."

#### 6.1. Types of Consultation

**Expert Consultation**:
- Experts en el campo revisan findings
- Identifican gaps o literatura missed
- Proveen context/interpretation

**Stakeholder Consultation**:
- Practitioners, policymakers, users
- Validan relevancia de findings
- Sugieren implications prácticas

#### 6.2. Methods

- **Workshops**: Focus groups con stakeholders
- **Interviews**: One-on-one con experts
- **Surveys**: Online questionnaire para broader input
- **Review Panels**: Formal review por advisory board

**Ejemplo del Paper**:
- Workshop con carers (n=15) para validar findings
- Identified additional grey literature sobre local services
- Provided practice-based insights no documentados en literatura

#### 6.3. Optional Nature

**Arksey & O'Malley Caveat**:
- Stage 6 es **optional** (diferencia con Stages 1-5 que son mandatory)
- Depende de: time, resources, purpose del scoping study

**When to Include**:
- ✅ Cuando findings informarán policy/practice
- ✅ Cuando stakeholder buy-in es crítico
- ✅ Cuando hay resources/time disponible

**When to Skip**:
- ⏭ Cuando scoping es purely academic (e.g., identifying research gaps)
- ⏭ Cuando time/budget limitado

## 4. Diferencias con Otros Tipos de Reviews

### 4.1. Scoping Study vs Systematic Review (Detallado)

**Systematic Review**:
- **Purpose**: Answer specific clinical/policy question
- **Question**: Narrow, well-defined (PICO format)
- **Search**: Exhaustive, all relevant databases
- **Selection**: Strict inclusion criteria, 2+ reviewers
- **Quality**: Critical appraisal mandatory (risk of bias tools)
- **Synthesis**: Meta-analysis (if possible) or narrative synthesis
- **Time**: 6-18 months típicamente
- **Protocol**: Pre-registered (PROSPERO), no deviation allowed
- **Output**: "Is intervention X effective?" → Yes/No/Uncertain + effect size

**Scoping Study**:
- **Purpose**: Map breadth/extent of literature
- **Question**: Broad, exploratory
- **Search**: Comprehensive pero not exhaustive
- **Selection**: Broader criteria, 1 reviewer OK
- **Quality**: No quality appraisal (typically)
- **Synthesis**: Descriptive/narrative
- **Time**: Weeks to 6 months
- **Protocol**: Flexible, iterative refinement allowed
- **Output**: "What is known about X?" → Themes, gaps, recommendations

### 4.2. Scoping Study vs Rapid Review

**Rapid Review**:
- Similar a Systematic Review pero con shortcuts para speed
- Timeframe: 4-12 weeks
- Shortcuts: Limited databases, no grey literature, single reviewer
- **Still focuses**: Specific question con quality appraisal

**Scoping Study**:
- No es "shortcut" de Systematic Review
- Different purpose: Mapping vs answering
- Timeframe comparable a Rapid Review
- Pero methodology es fundamentally different (exploratory)

### 4.3. Scoping Study vs Literature Review

**Literature Review** (narrative/traditional):
- No systematic search strategy
- Selection criteria unclear/implicit
- Synthesis is author's interpretation
- Risk of bias (cherry-picking)

**Scoping Study**:
- Systematic search strategy documented
- Explicit inclusion/exclusion criteria
- Transparent synthesis process
- Replicable methodology

**Key Difference**: Scoping Study es **systematic** (aunque exploratorio).

## 5. Evolución del Framework Post-2005

### 5.1. Levac et al. (2010) Enhancements

**Paper**: "Scoping studies: advancing the methodology"
**Journal**: Implementation Science
**Contribución**: Refinamientos basados en aplicar Arksey & O'Malley framework

**Recomendaciones Clave**:

1. **Research Question (Stage 1)**:
   - Clarificar "concept", "target population", "outcomes"
   - Usar team approach para definir pregunta (no solo solo researcher)

2. **Study Selection (Stage 3)**:
   - Incluir more than one reviewer (mejora reliability)
   - Pilot test inclusion criteria con 10-20 abstracts

3. **Data Charting (Stage 4)**:
   - Continually extract data y update charting form (iterative)
   - Team meetings para discutir emergent themes

4. **Reporting (Stage 5)**:
   - Clarify implications para future research, policy, practice
   - Use visual diagrams (concept maps, timelines)

5. **Consultation (Stage 6)**:
   - Treat como **mandatory** (not optional) cuando findings informan practice
   - Budget time/resources para consultation desde inicio

### 5.2. Peters et al. (2015) JBI Guidelines

**Paper**: "Guidance for conducting systematic scoping reviews"
**Journal**: International Journal of Evidence-Based Healthcare
**Contribución**: Joanna Briggs Institute (JBI) formalizó guidelines

**Adiciones al Framework**:

1. **Protocol Publication**:
   - Publicar scoping protocol a priori (like Systematic Review)
   - Increase transparency y reduce bias

2. **Quality Appraisal**:
   - Incluir quality appraisal cuando relevant
   - Useful para identificar high-quality studies para future synthesis

3. **Team Composition**:
   - Recomendar multidisciplinary team (content experts + methodologists)
   - Minimum 2 reviewers para selection/extraction

4. **Reporting Standards**:
   - Usar PRISMA-ScR extension (PRISMA for Scoping Reviews)
   - 20+ items checklist para reporting

### 5.3. PRISMA-ScR (2018)

**Paper**: "PRISMA Extension for Scoping Reviews (PRISMA-ScR)"
**Journal**: Annals of Internal Medicine
**Contribución**: Reporting guideline específico para Scoping Reviews

**22-Item Checklist**:
- Title: Identificar como "scoping review"
- Abstract: Structured (background, objectives, eligibility, sources, results)
- Introduction: Rationale + objectives
- Methods: Protocol, eligibility, information sources, search, selection, data charting, synthesis
- Results: Selection process, characteristics, results (by objective)
- Discussion: Summary, limitations, conclusions

**URL**: http://www.prisma-statement.org/Extensions/ScopingReviews

## 7. Ventajas y Limitaciones

### 7.1. Ventajas del Scoping Study Approach

✅ **Exploratory Nature**: Permite descubrir landscape sin presuponer findings
✅ **Breadth**: Cubre amplio rango de literatura vs deep dive
✅ **Flexibility**: Iterative refinement de research question
✅ **Speed**: Más rápido que Systematic Review (weeks vs months)
✅ **Gap Identification**: Ideal para identificar research gaps
✅ **Preliminary Work**: Informa future Systematic Reviews

### 7.2. Limitaciones

⚠️ **No Quality Appraisal**: No distingue high-quality vs low-quality evidence
⚠️ **No Meta-Analysis**: No sintetiza quantitative effect sizes
⚠️ **No Definitive Answers**: No responde "Does X work?" solo "What is known about X?"
⚠️ **Risk of Superficiality**: Breadth puede comprometer depth
⚠️ **Resource Intensive**: Aunque más rápido que SR, aún requiere significant effort

### 7.3. Críticas al Framework

**Crítica 1: "Too Similar to Systematic Review"**
- Algunos argumentan que proceso es casi tan riguroso como SR
- Response: Difference es en purpose (mapping vs answering), not rigor

**Crítica 2: "Lacks Quality Appraisal"**
- Sin quality assessment, puede incluir low-quality studies
- Response: Purpose es mapping, no synthesizing evidence quality

**Crítica 3: "Consultation Stage Vague"**
- Stage 6 es underdeveloped en paper original
- Response: Levac 2010 y JBI 2015 clarificaron guidelines

## 9. Mejores Prácticas (Evidence-Based)

### 9.1. Planning Phase

✅ **Define clear research question** (Stage 1):
- Broad enough para explorar, specific enough para guiar
- Revisit después de familiarization con 5-10 papers

✅ **Assemble multidisciplinary team**:
- Content expert + methodologist + librarian (para search)

✅ **Develop protocol**:
- Documentar plan a priori (aunque flexible)
- Include: question, inclusion criteria, search strategy

### 9.2. Execution Phase

✅ **Systematic search strategy** (Stage 2):
- Use Boolean operators (AND, OR, NOT)
- Search múltiples databases (no solo Google Scholar)
- Document search terms + results per database

✅ **Dual screening** (Stage 3):
- 2 reviewers independientes para title/abstract screening
- Reduce bias y improve reliability
- Resolve discrepancies por consensus

✅ **Pilot data charting** (Stage 4):
- Test charting form con 5-10 papers
- Refine form antes de charting todos

### 9.3. Reporting Phase

✅ **Use reporting guideline** (Stage 5):
- PRISMA-ScR checklist (22 items)
- Increase transparency y completeness

✅ **Include PRISMA flow diagram**:
- Visual de selection process
- Muestra números en cada stage (identification → screening → included)

✅ **Tabular + narrative synthesis**:
- Tablas para overview rápido
- Narrative para detail y context

### 9.4. Post-Publication

✅ **Make protocol + data available**:
- Publish protocol en Open Science Framework
- Share data extraction tables (if possible)

✅ **Update as needed**:
- Scoping studies pueden actualizarse conforme emerge nueva literatura
- "Living scoping review" concept
